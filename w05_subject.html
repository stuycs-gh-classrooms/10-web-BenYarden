How to play chess

Chess is a game with the goal of "checkmating" your opponents king based on tactical movement of all your pieces. Learning chess starts with understanding how pieces move

pawns move forwards one square at a time. They can move forward two if they have not yet moved. They can capture one unit forwards diagonally.

Rooks can move on any square horizontally or vertically

Knights move in an L shape going two squares vertically or horizontally, then one unit

Bishops move similar to rooks, but diagonally. They can't switch squares, meaning a bishop that starts on a white square will never access a black one

Queens can move as either a bishop or rook

Kings can move one square in any direction. They can not move to a square that is attacked by a piece from the other side.
